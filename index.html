How Forward Chaining Works

üìã What is a Rule-Based Expert System?

An expert system uses knowledge (rules) to make inferences about data (facts). It mimics the decision-making ability of a human expert.

‚ö° Forward Chaining

Forward chaining is a data-driven inference method where the engine starts with known facts and applies rules to derive new facts until no more rules can fire.

‚Üí

üîó Multi-Step Inference (Chaining)

Rules can chain together. The conclusion of one rule can trigger another rule. For example:

Rule 1: IF fever, cough THEN cold
Rule 2: IF cold, fatigue THEN flu
